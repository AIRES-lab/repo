Lab focus

Empowering security studies with artificial intelligence

AIRES Lab develops and evaluates AI-enabled methods for studying security problems where technical systems, institutions, information environments, and human behavior interact. The lab is especially interested in generative agents, experimental simulation, computational social science, and responsible AI use in security research.

Research areas

Where AIRES works

01

Generative Agents

Building controlled agent-based environments for security-relevant experimentation, scenario analysis, and theory development.

02

AI-Enabled Security Studies

Using machine learning and language models to extend how scholars analyze conflict, coercion, information operations, and strategic behavior.

03

Socio-Technical Risk

Examining how AI systems reshape institutions, decision-making, uncertainty, escalation, and accountability in security contexts.

04

Experimental Methods

Designing reproducible studies that combine simulation, qualitative insight, computational measurement, and human judgment.

Projects

Current directions

Agentic Security Simulation

Experimental environments for testing how generative agents behave under institutional constraints, uncertainty, and strategic incentives.

AI for Evidence Mapping

Tools for discovering, structuring, and comparing evidence across security studies, conflict research, and computational social science.

Information Operations and Digital Repression

Data-driven research on online manipulation, censorship, surveillance, and their relationship to political violence and armed conflict.
